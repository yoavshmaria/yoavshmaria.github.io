Keyword battleground

Where HSBC should defend, attack and avoid waste.

D

Defend hard

  • Brand + login: “hsbc login” is growing (+13.0%), and the homepage/auth pages drive the biggest high-intent organic volume.
  • Mortgage rates: HSBC is strong on “mortgage rates uk” and repayment calculator intent. Protect with schema, speed and CRO.
  • AI-answer content: HSBC is already strong in ChatGPT-sourced traffic. Defend answer authority before peers catch up.
A

Attack selectively

  • Mortgage calculator: HSBC captures only ~4.5% of tracked competitor click share on this huge intent pool.
  • Generic credit card intent: NatWest dominates. HSBC should only expand where it has sharper product/landing relevance.
  • Comparison surfaces: Push refreshed propositions into MSE, MoneySuperMarket, Experian, ClearScore and relevant broker tools.
C

Cut or constrain

  • Broad paid search: Current cost-per-visit is far above peers. Stop scaling until query hygiene and landing-page relevance improve.
  • Vanity volume: Lloyds shows that traffic volume without depth can create poor ROI.
  • Low-context display: Expand only after HSBC confirms high-intent publishers and retargeting segments are outperforming broad open-web inventory.

90-day action roadmap

What HSBC should do now, next and after that to improve ROI fast.

Days 1–30: Stop leakage

  • Split brand, generic, competitor and product campaigns in paid search.
  • Pause low-intent queries with weak cost-per-visit / low post-click depth.
  • Fix mobile UX on the top 10 traffic pages: homepage, auth, contact, online banking, mortgage pages.
  • Deploy strict display whitelist with finance-led publishers first.

Days 31–60: Rebuild quality demand

  • Launch AI-answer content hubs for mortgages, ISAs, cards, security, branch, international payments.
  • Refresh MSE / MoneySuperMarket / comparison copy, tables, offers and landing pages.
  • Improve next-step modules to lift pages/visit: calculators → AIP → product pages → apply.
  • Expand triggered CRM/email journeys where NatWest currently outscales HSBC.

Days 61–90: Scale what works

  • Reinvest only in channels proving lower cost-per-visit and stronger engagement.
  • Test mortgage calculator, premier and savings creative variants by audience and device.
  • Scale retargeting and high-intent contextual display once efficiency is proven.
  • Set executive dashboard targets: paid CPV, mobile page depth, GenAI visits, partner-share wins.

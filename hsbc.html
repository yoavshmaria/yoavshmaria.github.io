1
Critical gap
Activate NerdWallet & money.co.uk
Barclays generates 5,556 visits from NerdWallet and 4,386 from money.co.uk. HSBC has zero presence on both. These are high-intent research audiences actively comparing financial products. Priority placement on mortgage, current account, and credit card comparison pages would capture prospects mid-decision.
2
High impact
Launch Rightmove mortgage display
Rightmove's home buyer audience is the highest-intent mortgage prospect pool in the UK. Barclays is already present. HSBC's mortgage tools already rank well organically — a Rightmove display campaign would complete the funnel by reaching buyers at the point of property search, before they've chosen a lender.
3
Medium impact
Add uSwitch and Experian
uSwitch audiences are actively switching financial products — ideal for HSBC current account and credit card offers. Experian users are credit-score aware and actively shopping. Both publishers are used by Barclays. Adding them improves acquisition quality vs the irrelevant gaming/misc sites in HSBC's current publisher tail.
4
Medium impact
Maintain MSE & MoneySuperMarket
HSBC's strongest publisher channels (MSE 37.4%, MoneySuperMarket 26.3%) are both declining year-on-year. Invest in maintaining editorial presence, sponsoring product comparison tables, and securing featured listings — especially for savings accounts and mortgages during rate-change cycles.
5
Quick win
Activate finder.com for international
NatWest generates 572 visits from finder.com — heavily used by expats. HSBC's international account is its #1 paid search page. Extending to finder.com display placements reaches the same audience in a research-first environment where HSBC has a clear product advantage over domestic competitors.
6
Quick win · Free
Block low-quality publisher tail
HSBC's display publisher list includes bloxd.io (gaming), typing.com, elvebredd.com, topsportfeed.com, and papasgaming.com — irrelevant audiences with near-zero conversion intent. Blocking these immediately redirects budget to quality publishers with no increase in total spend.

Top ROI Levers

Priority-ranked growth opportunities based on competitive gap analysis

ROI lever prioritisation matrix
Impact vs effort — bubble size represents estimated monthly traffic uplift
01
Critical · Paid Search
Launch mortgage paid search — immediately
HSBC has zero paid search on mortgages. Barclays spends ~£43.5K/month (29K clicks), NatWest ~£61.5K (20K clicks). Target: "mortgage calculator", "mortgage agreement in principle", "best mortgage rates UK", "first-time buyer mortgage". Est. uplift: +20–30K paid visits/month at parity.
02
Critical · Paid Search
Conquer current account switching terms
Barclays' #1 paid page generates 55.3K clicks on current account terms — HSBC has no equivalent. "Switch bank account", "best current account UK", "bank account with rewards" represent direct acquisition at measurable CPA. The 7-day switch guarantee makes this highly competitive on conversion rate.
03
High · Display
Activate NerdWallet + money.co.uk display
Barclays gets 5,556 visits from NerdWallet and 4,386 from money.co.uk — both zero for HSBC. Research-first, high-intent audiences actively comparing financial products. Estimated +8–10K high-quality visits/month from these two publishers alone.
04
High · Display
Rightmove mortgage display campaign
100M+ monthly home buyer and seller visits — the highest-intent mortgage audience in the UK. Barclays is already present. HSBC's mortgage tools rank organically for "mortgage calculator". A Rightmove display campaign completes the funnel at the point of property search.
05
High · Paid Search
Scale ISA + investments paid (Jan–April)
HSBC runs 472 clicks/month on ISA keywords vs Barclays' 4,302 (9× gap). April tax year-end is peak ISA season — campaigns should scale significantly from January. Target: "stocks & shares ISA 2025", "best cash ISA", "ISA allowance". Highest-value asset management acquisition window.
06
Quick Win · Free
Block irrelevant display publishers
HSBC's publisher tail includes gaming sites (bloxd.io, papasgaming.com), typing.com, and sports feeds — audiences with zero financial intent. Blocking these immediately reallocates impressions to quality publishers at no extra cost. This is the fastest free efficiency gain available.

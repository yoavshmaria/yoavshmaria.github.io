Executive summary

monday.com is operating from a position of brand strength and high-engagement product interest. The brand wins in paid search scale versus Smartsheet and Asana, competes aggressively in competitor conquesting, and maintains premium on-site engagement. The biggest ROI unlock is not “more spend everywhere.” It is reallocating spend from broad comparison-heavy paid traffic into topic-led organic authority, AI-era SERP capture, and differentiated creative that translates monday.com from a generic “work platform” into specific business outcomes by role and use case.

Channel mix: strong direct engine, under-leveraged diversification

Traffic is heavily direct across the category. That is good for brand equity, but dangerous when paired with very high PPC outlay and a weak organic non-brand share.

Traffic source share across the category (last 3 months)
What the source data says
  • Direct dominates category demand, roughly 77.5% of tracked share. monday.com already captures a large portion of this.
  • Paid search share is led by ClickUp and monday.com. monday.com contributes ~38.7% of paid search origin share among peers shown.
  • Organic search share trails Smartsheet’s leadership, suggesting room to replace expensive clicks with durable topic authority.
  • Display is currently more visible for ClickUp in the data, creating a creative white space for monday.com in upper-funnel visual storytelling.
ROI move
Shift 10–15% of conquest and broad-match budget into organic topic clusters, high-intent comparison content, and paid remarketing tied to engaged sessions. This will lower blended CAC over 2–3 quarters.

SEO & topic authority

The SEO story is split: ranking distribution improved dramatically through late 2025, but organic click concentration remains brand-heavy. monday.com should convert its product breadth into topic breadth.

SEO strengths
  • Brand ranking share surged into top positions.

  • SERP visibility aligns well with AI overviews, related questions, images, and video surfaces.
SEO weaknesses
  • Pricing and CRM topic clicks declined despite clear commercial intent.

Most important SEO recommendation
Build a topic-led growth engine, not a keyword-led publishing calendar. Own clusters around planning systems, cross-functional visibility, CRM workflows, templates, prioritization frameworks, dashboards, and migration. Every topic should ladder into a product module, template, or role landing page.

Paid search intelligence

monday.com is one of the most aggressive paid players in the category. The opportunity is better message architecture and tighter spend discipline by intent layer.

Spend dependency risk
PPC spend vs Smartsheet (USD, total)
Where monday.com is playing hardest
  • Brand and competitor terms: Asana, ClickUp, Trello, Notion.
  • Broad utility terms with huge volume: Google Sheets, Google Calendar, spreadsheets-related intent.
  • CRM conquesting: HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zoho, HighLevel.
Main paid-search inefficiency
Ads frequently repeat a generic claim: “monday.com is so much better” or broad “AI project manager” language. That is serviceable for comparison intent, but weak for high-CPC categories where buyers need differentiated proof, role fit, migration ease, and time-to-value.
Paid search messaging gaps
Gap 1: Too much comparative bravado, not enough quantified business value.
Gap 2: Broad “AI” messaging is not tied to a role-specific pain point.
Gap 3: Spreadsheet-intent ads often promise project management, but should bridge from familiarity to systemization.
Gap 4: CRM conquest pages need stronger migration and admin-proof messaging versus HubSpot/Salesforce/Pipedrive.
Recommended paid-search restructure
  • Bucket A: Brand defense — protect branded + high-conversion sitelinks, but cap excess overlap with organic.
  • Bucket B: Competitor switch intent — use migration, admin effort, adoption speed, and reporting proof.
  • Bucket C: Category demand creation — focus on use case pages, not generic homepage traffic.
  • Bucket D: Spreadsheet escape velocity — message the transition from manual sheets to live collaborative workflows.

Biggest growth gaps and ROI opportunities

These are the highest-confidence areas where monday.com can improve blended marketing ROI.

Gap A — Too much money on broad paid utility intent
Heavy clicks around spreadsheet/calendar utility can scale traffic, but often without enough commercial specificity. Keep the volume, but route it to tighter “why switch” narratives and stronger lead capture offers.
Gap B — Organic non-brand education is underbuilt
Competitors win on frameworks, methodology, and practical templates. monday.com should own the operating system behind how teams plan, prioritize, and execute.
Gap C — Comparison pages are overused as a generic paid weapon
Comparison landing pages need more role-specific switch arguments, migration plans, product proof, and pricing clarity. General superiority claims are insufficient.
Gap D — AI messaging is broad, not operational
“AI platform” is memorable, but buyers need specifics: summarize work, surface blockers, recommend next steps, generate dashboards, automate status, score leads, and flag risk.

90-day playbook

A practical roadmap to improve efficiency without sacrificing growth.

Days 1–30
  • Audit top paid landing pages by intent and conversion depth.
  • Reduce spend on lowest-converting broad spreadsheet terms by 10–15%.
  • Rewrite competitor ad sets around migration, adoption, and reporting proof.
  • Launch AI-answer-ready content templates for top commercial topics.
Days 31–60
  • Publish topic clusters: planning frameworks, calendar systems, budget templates, PMO, CRM setup, dashboard use cases.
  • Launch 5–7 role-based display variants.
  • Stand up engaged-session retargeting with proof-led creative.
  • Create “spreadsheet to workflow” and “switch from X” lifecycle emails.
Days 61–90
  • Scale what converts: role pages, CRM modules, Gantt, dashboard, templates.
  • Expand Reddit/YouTube content loops.
  • Introduce interactive ROI calculators on category and comparison pages.
  • Shift another 5% of spend from generic conquesting to proven high-intent content + remarketing.
North-star recommendation
Turn monday.com from a brand people know into a decision architecture people trust: educational topic → proof-rich use case → role-specific page → migration/ROI proof → trial/demo.

Executive summary

Similarweb has strong brand demand and high visibility in the US, but its traffic base, repeat behavior, and depth of engagement have weakened meaningfully over the last 24 months. Competitors—especially Semrush and Ahrefs—are winning on two fronts: deeper product exploration and broader acquisition across non-brand problem/solution topics. The biggest ROI unlock is not “more of the same” brand capture. It is a deliberate shift toward high-intent category messaging, retention loops, and comparison-led paid and content programs.

Primary growth thesis
Move budget from brand defense to problem-solution acquisition + activation
1. Fix acquisition mix

Paid and organic exposure are too dependent on brand and competitor-name intercepts instead of broader decision-stage topics like traffic intelligence, ad intelligence, app intelligence, and market benchmarking.

2. Raise engagement quality

Similarweb’s pages/visit and duration lag peers. The likely issue is landing pages selling features without building “next click momentum” into tools, templates, use cases, and proof experiences.

3. Build stronger retention loops

Returning users are low relative to new users. Weekly insights, alerts, saved watchlists, category dashboards, and competitor monitoring should become the core of lifecycle marketing.

SEO: topic strategy needs quality control and ICP focus

Ranking distribution improved in higher positions, but total keyword universe shrank and many winning topic clusters appear low-fit for B2B monetization.

Fix topic hygiene

SEO verdict

Major issue

Organic topic clusters include adult/streaming/adjacent lookup topics. These may drive sessions, but they likely dilute brand trust, intent quality, and sales efficiency.

Better direction

Build topic clusters around buyer jobs: competitor benchmarking, market sizing, channel share analysis, app intelligence, ecommerce category analysis, lead generation intelligence, and investor research.

SERP reality

AI Overviews and People Also Ask are rising. Similarweb should publish “answer-first” assets and proof-rich pages to win those surfaces.

Best-fit topic opportunities

Website traffic intelligence

Keep owning it, but reframe from “checker” to “decision intelligence”: channels, competitors, audience overlap, and conversion clues.

Competitor ad analysis

Strong paid cluster exists. Expand organic content around ad libraries, creative themes, landing page teardowns, and budget trend analysis.

Market and category benchmarking

Top websites, category leaders, and regional leaders can become repeatable acquisition hubs with strong enterprise intent.

App intelligence and rankings

Paid data already hints at traction here. Build category pages for app marketers and mobile strategy teams.

Alternative / comparison journeys

Pages comparing Similarweb to Semrush, Ahrefs, SpyFu, Sensor Tower, RocketReach, Jungle Scout should be full-funnel assets, not simple feature tables.

SEO actions that increase pipeline quality

  1. Audit and de-prioritize low-fit topic clusters that attract curiosity traffic with low B2B conversion potential.
  2. Build topic hubs around business questions, not generic keyword variants: “Which competitors are gaining share?”, “Where are rivals buying traffic?”, “What categories are growing fastest?”
  3. Create answer-first pages optimized for AI Overview inclusion with charts, definitions, methodology, and data snippets.
  4. Add deeper internal journeys from top acquisition pages into product demos, templates, category reports, and saved-watchlist workflows.
  5. Turn top-websites and market leaderboard pages into email capture and alert subscription engines.
